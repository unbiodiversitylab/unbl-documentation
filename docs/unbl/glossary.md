# Glossary

## Basemap {#basemap}
The background map layer that provides geographic context, showing features like country boundaries, terrain, or satellite imagery. Users can toggle between grayscale and satellite basemap options.

## Biodiversity Intactness Index {#bii}
A measure of how intact local biodiversity is relative to a pre-human baseline, expressed as a percentage. Values closer to 100% indicate ecosystems that are more similar to their natural state.

## CBD Secretariat {#cbd-sec}
The Secretariat of the Convention on Biological Diversity, the international treaty governing global biodiversity conservation efforts.

## Clip and Export {#clip-export}
A feature that allows users to download spatial data cropped to a specific geographic boundary, such as a country or administrative region.

## Coordinate System {#crs}
A reference framework used to define the positions of points on Earth's surface. Common systems include latitude/longitude (geographic coordinates) and various projected coordinate systems.

## Country Boundaries {#boundaries}
Administrative borders that define the territorial limits of nations, used in UNBL to provide country-specific data and metrics.

## Data App {#data-app}
The interactive mapping application component of UNBL where users can visualize and analyze spatial data layers.

## Data Layers {#data-layers}
Individual datasets that can be visualized on the map, each representing different environmental, social, or economic variables. Examples include forest cover, protected areas, and population density.

## Digital Public Good (DPG) {#dpg}
Open-source data, software, or content that advances sustainable development and is freely available for use. In UNBL, DPG layers are marked with special recognition for their open accessibility.

## Dynamic Metrics {#dynamic-metrics}
Real-time or regularly updated indicators calculated from spatial data that provide at-a-glance statistics for specific geographic areas, such as tree cover loss percentages or biodiversity indices.

## Enhanced Vegetation Index (EVI) {#evi}
A satellite-based measurement of vegetation health and density that improves upon the Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) by reducing atmospheric and soil background influences.

## Fire Activity {#fire-activity}
Satellite-detected fire events and burn scars, typically measured monthly to track wildfire patterns and forest burning activities.

## GIS {#gis}
Geographic Information System - technology used to capture, store, analyze, and display geographically referenced data.

## Global Land Cover {#lulc}
Classification of Earth's surface into different categories such as forests, croplands, urban areas, and water bodies, typically derived from satellite imagery.

## Human Industrial Footprint {#human-footprint}
A measure of human (industrial-level) influence on the environment, combining factors like population density, infrastructure, agriculture, and land use change to assess cumulative human impact.

## Jurisdictions {#jurisdiction}
Administrative or legal regions with defined boundaries, including countries, states, provinces, and other governmental territories.

## Layer Overlay {#overlay}
The arrangement and stacking order of multiple data layers on the map, where users can control which layers appear on top and how they interact visually.

## Legend {#legend}
The map key that explains the symbols, colors, and patterns used to represent different values or categories in the displayed data layers.

## Map View {#map-view}
The main interactive interface displaying geographic data, including controls for zooming, panning, and layer management.

## Metadata {#metadata}
Descriptive information about datasets, including source, methodology, temporal coverage, spatial resolution, and data quality assessments.

## Opacity {#opacity}
The transparency level of data layers, which can be adjusted to allow underlying layers to show through while maintaining visibility of the top layer.

## Place Labels {#place-labels}
Text annotations on the map showing names of geographic features like countries, cities, states, and landmarks.

## Projection {#projection}
A mathematical method for representing the curved surface of Earth on a flat map, with different projections optimizing for different properties like area, distance, or direction. Related to [Coordinate Reference Systems](#crs)

## Protected Areas {#protected-areas}
Legally designated regions managed for biodiversity conservation, including national parks, nature reserves, and other conservation areas as defined by the World Database on Protected Areas (WDPA).

## Raster Data {#raster-data}
Spatial data represented as a grid of cells or pixels, where each cell contains a value representing information about that location, commonly used for satellite imagery and environmental variables.

## Resolution {#resolution}
The level of detail in spatial data, typically measured as the size of the smallest unit (pixel) that can be distinguished, expressed in meters (e.g., 30m resolution means each pixel represents a 30×30 meter area).

## Satellite View {#satellite-view}
A basemap option showing satellite or aerial imagery of Earth's surface, providing visual context for data analysis.

## Spatial Data {#spatial-data}
Information that has a geographic component, meaning it can be mapped and analyzed in relation to specific locations on Earth.

## Terrestrial Carbon Density {#terr-carbon-density}
The amount of carbon stored in above and below-ground biomass and soil per unit area, typically measured in tons per hectare.

## Time Series {#time-series}
Data collected at multiple time points, allowing users to visualize and analyze changes over time through animations or by selecting specific years or date ranges.

## Transboundary Areas {#transboudnary-area}
Regions that span across national borders, often referring to shared ecosystems, watersheds, or conservation areas managed cooperatively by multiple countries.

## Tree Cover Loss {#tree-cover-loss}
The removal or mortality of tree canopy cover, measured annually using satellite data to track deforestation and forest degradation patterns.

## UN Biodiversity Lab (UNBL){#unbl}
A collaborative platform that democratizes access to spatial data and analytic tools to support evidence-based decision-making for conservation and sustainable development.

## UNDP {#undp}
United Nations Development Programme - the UN agency focused on sustainable development and poverty reduction.

## UNEP {#unep}
United Nations Environment Programme - the UN agency responsible for coordinating environmental activities and assisting countries in implementing environmentally sound policies.

## UNEP-WCMC {#unep-wcmc}
UN Environment Programme World Conservation Monitoring Centre - the specialist biodiversity assessment centre of UNEP that provides information services on conservation and sustainable use of species and ecosystems.

## Vector Data {#vector-data}
Spatial data represented using geometric shapes (points, lines, and polygons) to represent geographic features, commonly used for boundaries, roads, and discrete locations.

## Workspace {#workspace}
A secure, password-protected collaborative environment within UNBL where institutions can upload, share, and analyze their own datasets alongside global data layers.

## Zoom {#zoom-level}
The ability to change the scale of the map view to show more detail (zoom in) or a broader geographic area (zoom out), controlled through map interface buttons or mouse interactions.
